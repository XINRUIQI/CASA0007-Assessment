# Digital Exclusion Risk Mapping in London

This project explores digital exclusion across London at LSOA level. It combines indicators related to digital participation, deprivation, and broadband conditions to identify neighbourhoods that may be at higher risk of digital exclusion.

The main aim is to move beyond a simple “poor internet = digital exclusion” explanation. By comparing multiple dimensions, the project shows that some areas may have relatively good connectivity but still face low levels of digital participation or other forms of disadvantage.

## Data
The analysis uses a range of secondary datasets, including:
- ONS Digital Propensity Index (DPI)
- IMD and related socio-economic indicators
- Ofcom broadband data
- London LSOA boundary data

## Methods
The main methods used in this project include:
- data cleaning and joining at LSOA level
- Principal Component Analysis (PCA)
- k-means clustering
- composite risk score construction (ICS)
- comparison with benchmark measures
- basic spatial analysis

## Key findings
The results suggest that digital exclusion is spatially uneven across London and cannot be explained by broadband access alone. Different neighbourhood types show different combinations of risk, which means that policy responses may need to be more targeted and place-specific.
